Drill down: human flourishing

From Challenge to Flourishing

This screen makes the case for change: Year 8 engagement often falls when learning feels less relevant, less relational and less connected to agency.

The challenge

Students ask, "Why am I learning this?"

Academic pressure, reduced autonomy, lower relevance and the "in-between" nature of Year 8 can combine into disengagement.

Research response

Engagement is relational, purposeful and active.

The recurring moves are smaller learning communities, stronger teacher-student relationships, student voice, authentic learning and early identification.

The gap

Current systems are good at history, weaker at diagnosis.

Grades, attendance and completion tell us what has happened. The diagnostic needs to help teachers decide what should happen next.

Executive proposition To help learners explore, connect and create, we first need to know what will unlock growth.

Drill down: knowing the learner

Knowing the Learner

Achievement alone is not enough. Two students can share the same result and need very different support.

What flourishing learners need

Core argument Same achievement can hide different needs. The point is not to replace achievement data, but to understand who the learner is becoming and what support they need next.
Research base Engagement, persistence and growth are shaped by more than ability. The poster connects the model to self-efficacy, self-determination, belonging, expectancy-value, mastery learning and adolescent engagement research.

Drill down: diagnostic model

The ECC Learner Diagnostic

The diagnostic connects existing data, learner voice, teacher insight and wellbeing information into a whole learner profile.

Diagnostic question

What is most likely to unlock growth next?

The aim is not to rank students or create fixed labels. It is to identify the primary growth lever for action.

Drill down: learning intelligence

Learning Intelligence

This screen reframes information as decision support: who needs what, when and why?

Big shift

From "What happened?" to "What should happen next?"

Learning intelligence uses observation, learner voice and learning evidence to identify the next best action for growth.

Complete learner picture

Observation + learner voice + learning evidence.

Teacher observations, what learners tell us and what learning experiences reveal need to be read together.

Learner signals

Challenge, Confidence, Purpose, Ownership and Connection.

The signals help teachers ask whether the learner is stretched, supported, motivated, agentic and known.

Decision cycle

Experience -> evidence -> insight -> diagnosis -> action -> growth.

The dashboard should make this cycle visible enough for teachers to respond earlier and more consistently.

Big idea Learning Intelligence is not about collecting more information. It is about making better decisions.

Teacher Moves

  • Open with a purpose question before the task.
  • Name the real-world use of the skill.
  • Connect feedback to growth, not just correction.

Learning Design Moves

  • Use a real audience or client problem.
  • Offer product choice with shared success criteria.
  • Add an industry, community or future pathway link.

Review Evidence

  • Short learner reflection after each sprint.
  • Teacher observation of participation quality.
  • Compare engagement across task types.
